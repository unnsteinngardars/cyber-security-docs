This is the V2 split of the CCDCOE IDFC cheat sheet — one sub-page per topic so each section can grow without the scroll getting ridiculous. The series follows the order the course actually taught the material in: acquire the evidence first, then peel back layers from RAM down to filesystem metadata, then per-hive registry work, then user-activity artefacts.

Every section follows the same shape: what artefact, where it lives, how to extract it with the listed tools, how to parse it, why it matters forensically, and what to cross-reference it against. No single artefact is convincing on its own — corroboration across two or three independent artefacts is what turns a finding into a defensible conclusion.
